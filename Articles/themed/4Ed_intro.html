Introduction

Last edited: 7/4/2022

Yet again, welcome to the 4th edition of The Spark Magazine. As you begin to read this edition, you can check out our other editions out just to have a feel of what we are about. If you already have, I would like you to have a moment of self introspection right now. Ask yourself, “What will it take?” Maybe to live the life you want to live; to have the state of mind you desire; or maybe to have the relationships you need. I am certain that each one of us have dreams and desires and probably have read a couple of motivational quotes on Instagram that made us believe that we are capable of having it. Although, when we look into our lives, we are not doing that great. You might have been told that you are doing great but you know deep within where you are versus where you want to be. Presumably, you do not have goals at all and are waiting for your hour of death. This could be because you are numb to aspiration and it is much easier that way.

In this edition we are attempting to remind everyone of the necessity for them to pursue their goals, dreams or healthy desires. It is not required for everyone to sit down and plan and introspect about what their really want. Intuitively, there is something we feel we should do at a moment, for example, greeting a person, an investment, a run, or inevitably planning. Our brains already know what we want and if we would not filter out or procrastinate those small tasks we are aware that our lives could be a little bit better. So why fear? Why procrastinate? Why hesitate? At the end of the day you know that you want it.

"In this edition we are attempting to remind everyone of the necessity for them to pursue their goals, dreams or healthy desires."

Imagine waking up each day, brushing your teeth and taking a shower. For the 3rd month you have been consistently waking up on time. You have put enough effort to make each day a masterpiece. I believe that if you master your day, you master your week, then your month, your year, decade, unavoidably your life. Our days seem so insignificant that we throw them away, regularly, because we have another day called tomorrow. So why worry? We should worry because our days are building blocks of our lives. Imagine each day to be like each genetic code: it defines your life just like genes define how a person is. If one of the genes are damaged, a person can be easily susceptible to the worst of diseases. Even a momentary life. Is this the day that will be problematic your whole life (the bad gene)?

In order for one to exponentiate their probability of stumbling into success, if I may, there is an urgent need to embrace focus. Everyday we are clouded by the need for our dopamine to be spiked. My assumption is that we are more distracted than past generations. I might be wrong but I think that across the board we must aim to have moment occupied by only one task – nothing else. That is difficult but beneficial. Our phones and other gadgets are detrimental to our success at the initial stages. Discipline is the driving force in ensuring complete focus on our work. Thereafter our focus should be on the right task, not just any task.

Sometimes it is easier to do all the above mentioned things whilst we put off what is fleetingly critical. We hide from the necessary task like prey from a predator. We procrastinate productively. Rather we do everything than that specific task. It makes sense because of the fear that it stirs inside us. One of the fundamental ways to know what to do is asking ourselves, “What do I fear the most?” and do it. Obviously we should not be naïve in our approach because some fears are necessary. Though, in most cases facing fear is the best option. Fear is our roadmap to freedom. Ultimately, to our success. Execution of this concept is also delicate: we need to move direct and quickly before we think too much about it.

In continuation, we ought to take immediate action. It would bring greater joy if you decided to let go of this magazine right now to start, continue and/or finish an important task you have right now. Like. Right. Now! But do not feel bad because, by chance, reading this magazine could be that task. Even if it is not, that does not mean beat yourself up about not being able to today – although, I would rather you execute the task – live to fight another day.

There are more tips and they are endless but to focus the introduction to what is important. As said in previous magazines, I believe that emaSwati should also focus on themselves. Because in our aim to become an ideal country we ought to be tough enough to put in the work. Just like the name, our aim is to ignite the spark of love for the nation despite and in spite of the conditions. To emphasize, it will take us to see it through, being the driving force for change in the country. We ought to strive with our blood and tears because it must happen. As you continue to read the magazine, focus on what you can do, how your dreams and goals are going to bring a positive change to liSwati, emaSwati and the country as a whole.
