It takes courage to work smart.

Last edited: 22/01/2023

Following the article on the educational section of the second edition, there is a necessity to dive in further into the mindset of approaching school. I truly believe that people are frustrated by school, and for now it is the only path I think should be taken by people who aren’t creative enough to live outside of school – including me. Although, I believe education should be a human enriching experience that helps people satisfy their curiosity not a commodity forced on to other people for survival. But that’s a topic for another day. This article also applies to people who are not in school. One of my instructors, who happened to be my advisor too, said to me, “I know you are a hard worker… but you need to start working smart.” Meanwhile, that day I was not feeling good at all, we had had a difficult conversation of how I am at the brink of failing. As she talked to me, I zoned out of the room and her voice became an echo as I replayed all the days I had ploughed through her assignment for hours, to no end. I thought that she must be kidding me, I have been losing sleep and sacrificing my other classes for this class, and all she had to say was that that class was easy; that I am spending more time than I should; or that I should work smart. My first thought was, “Am I dumb?” With proper explanation, at the end of the conversation I surrendered, the obvious choice was to change the way I think about her class, school and that completely obliterated my ego. Hard work always beats no work, but working smart surpasses them both! She was right, and my main takeaway was that it takes courage to work smart.

Definitely something had to change, and drastically! Spending a lot of time on one thing would be unsustainable and an awful strategy towards success. However, I had a void: what would be my new strategy? It was going to be working smart! Working smart can be a feel-good phrase for some and it may imply the independence from hard work and that could be extremely deceiving. Sometimes working smart is working hard and no other way. The essential part of working smart is identifying the requirements and the proper strategies needed to accomplish the task at hand.

What I did after that interaction is what determined my trajectory. I went back to the drawing board and decided to take a new approach to not only that course but my other classes and life. Things had to change or else. I started with a reflection by asking myself what I thought I was doing right, what I thought I was doing absolutely wrong and everything in between. Goal setting is fundamental in the way I function, so I had to write down my new goals. These included increasing focus and asking more questions which I thought would inevitably award better marks than what I was getting. Scheduling and goal setting are mutually inclusive. I planned the previous assignments that went terribly wrong, and I had to fix, and set exactly when I will be able to fix them. Ultimately, a positive attitude was essential in maintaining steady progress. Motivation was at an all-time low, but I had to keep pressing on. I was hopeful too: I thought that it would be essential to show up until the end. What was also crucial in my everyday life was maintaining composure, being relaxed helped me focus on one thing. This was not just an overnight transformation, but you can be shocked the resources one has if they have necessity, discipline and the courage to take a new perspective of learning. It takes courage to allow your beliefs to be challenged and furthermore – if you find the concept plausible – to adopt it. It takes courage to allow your ego to be set aside in order to attain an impeccable final product.

People, especially in academia, have a toxic mindset of being individualistic. If a person has been told that they are smart or on the other extreme, that they are stupid, they adopt a culture of being individualistic. They think education comes naturally, and they will be able to figure it out themselves. Even the smartest person had to learn how to write their name at some point. There was a period in their lives where they had to scribble and draw shapes that even today they cannot repeat because there was no pattern, thought process or intellectual inference. They were just incapable of writing. The social media age has perpetuated the mindset of showing our best side and hiding our worst side. We are in an illusion that life can or will be perfect (some people do not do this with school, but in other areas of their lives). There is a façade brought up in attempt to appear “fine” in our own metrics of what fine is. It is hard to ask for help, or we choose to figure things out for ourselves. We slowly die inside until it is too late to recover. We need the courage to collaborate and eliminate the amount of time we spend studying and figuring things out.

Linked with the previous point, we as people hardly take advantage of the fact that people have achieved what we wish to achieve. Mentors are one of the highly effective assets we could have as we climb the ladder to our predetermined lives. It is very hard to do things ourselves. We need to be able to find someone who we can learn from, who can offer their perspectives as they know what works. It is true that things that work for one may not work for other people, but the chances are high enough. And mentors provide more than just advice, but they offer a key to success, which is accountability. If we are accountable to someone, we are more likely to do a particular task. Mentors are people you value and respect. It could be a colleague, your instructor, other people in academia and parents. Mentors offer support and enforce discipline among their mentees. Bottom line is that an effective mentor is someone who you have enough respect that you do what they say and put aside your ego. It takes courage to go out of your way and connect with people that could assist you. It takes courage to listen and to be coachable. Furthermore, it takes courage to work smart.

Ask questions. Always. Ask them during class as you acquire information or ask after class. Asking questions significantly reduces the amount of extra studying you need to do. Our instructors or teacher are there to be challenged by our series of questions. The state in which you truly understand a subject is one of the most blissful states one could be in and we tend to not experience it. It is very distressing that there are people who complete a course and they still do not get anything. They are just confused about their class. Learning has been humanity’s greatest strength and a confused mind is a stressed mind. Our curiosity keeps on increasing if we are actually learning. There are people who exist who stopped learning years ago but they now survive from class to class with no hope to ever be curious and that is apathetic. You can ask questions to a number of people: librarians, students and teaching assistants. It is important and time savvy. Take courage and work smart!

Part of working smart demands the discipline of not skipping a step. When you have a project, fundamentally, you need to break it down to actionable steps that are laid out linearly, one after the other. Take for example, if one needs to write an essay they tend to start writing the first draft which is the biggest mistake. That cannot produce a masterpiece. Especially because that first draft is usually written hours before the deadline. My instructor, the one I refer to earlier on, gave me this insight. Well, I cannot say it is an insight because it has been my life for the past 2 decades. That being said, the devil is in the details. She clearly insisted that when you tackle a small chunk of the whole project, your aim is to make the next step to be way easier than the one you are doing. For example, plan well enough to accommodate lazy days and unforeseen events. Read and take notes that will be easy to use. With all this care and discipline, the next step is inevitably simple. No one, however, can be this disciplined if they do not have a mentor or ask questions whether they are on the right track. This long process is impossible if you still try to do it by yourself or are still going to figure it out on your own. Asking a question in computer class could potentially save you 2 hours of trial and error. That is enough to write at least 2 pages of an essay. Think about it. Why do you need to work long and hard?. It takes courage to weigh your options and take on the path least travelled, which is starting early and being efficient in your approach. Starting on time gives you an ample amount of time to refine your work, and as the saying goes, “Wine improves with age.”

I think this has happened only to me, for a long time I had been confused by what we were doing in my advisor's class. I just did the wrong assignments and did not even know what is due when. Likewise, I was just winging it. I am embarrassed thinking about that class because I just did not do the basic step, which was reading and understanding the tasks. This point is similar to the previous point, but I must emphasize it. Before you even proceed with planning, breaking down the task or taking necessary actions – understand the task. Clear out everything that you do not understand by simply asking questions. There is no other way. Work the instructions thoroughly before you start the task. You could translate it, you can write it in your own words, or you could annotate it or anything but make sure it is clear what you are supposed to do. You can even ask for strategies of how to go about it – if need be. Once you clear that out, you can start working on it. Ensure you know the main requirements and what skills are graded. Be clear in your head, then the whole process will be smoother. Prepare yourself first, do not just jump right in. This could differ in other areas of your life, but the fundamentals are knowing what is being required and what is important in achieving good results.

As stated above, working smart is not synonymous with working easy because sometimes you need to get your hands dirty and do the unpleasurable tasks. I have already highlighted that working smart could also mean working hard and as long as you realize that is the strategy that is required in a particular instance. You need to do the tasks that most people productively procrastinate. You may even spend more time than what most people do but having two sets of notes where everyone has one, but this is a strategy. It is deliberate action. One set of notes are just random things that you thought could be useful, and they are layout according to the sequence of the book. The secondary notes could be those which are polished and structured in a way that suits your essay. You may also need to follow the rules of writing and even, in some cases, evaluate each sentence. But if you have a clear direction and plan, you might as well have every task done accordingly. It takes courage to persevere in the laborious tasks.

In conclusion, we could ask ourselves, “How could I learn?” The simple answer is that we could work smart. Working smart, gives you the optimal results at a fraction of time. Working smart is not as easy as it is portrayed, but it is necessary. If some of the principles highlighted above can be translated to other areas in life – where they are applicable – one should do so. It is time for emaSwati to strive into working smart so that they get to achieve better results. It is time for you to take courage by learning the principle of working smart.
