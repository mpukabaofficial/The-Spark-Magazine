Vision 2022: Humble and Attainable | Part 3

Last edited: 8/9/2022

Why are you reading part 3, the finale, of the Vision 2022 trilogy? I thought I was outrageous enough on the first two parts hoping no one reads this one. My thoughts were: “I am going to make the pores so small that they filter out most readers.” Especially, I had the guts, and I still do, to proclaim a new vision for eSwatini. I thought it would be easy for everyone to think, “Okay, that’s it!” As a consequence, disappearing in their caves because it is just a dream and eSwatini can never amount to anything.

"Especially, I had the guts, and I still do, to proclaim a new vision for eSwatini. "

However, I am sure you are going to be glad that you have read this part because the mindset I am encouraging emaSwati to start having is completely vital in any pursuit of excellence. Truth be told, I know I am not an authority figure in eSwatini in any way. The problem is, I was waiting for someone within our leadership to say something to challenge and encourage us, as a nation, to pursue the greater good for our country. I became too impatient because I value time and I can never agree to wasting it. But what did I mean really when I said, “Vision eSwatini”, and what is expected of emaSwati? I meant let us play with our own rules and utilize our strengths in ensuring that we are the best that we can be.

My assumptions when I was told eSwatini would be a developed country by 2022 were distorted, immature, and stereotypical. For me, nothing meant “first world” than an impossibly tall skyscraper. A marvel that everyone was going to see when they realized they could not see its pinnacle. It meant a fully technological operated society, basically a utopia. It meant everything that America is, and to have all that was absolute bliss! The more I think about it, I realize that I was just hooked on a fantasy and forgot the real world - some call it detachment from reality. I am positive that their description is precise. I think the fuss that I have about the 2022 dream being cast off is rooted in my selfishness and unhealthy fantasy of how things could be. Or maybe that’s not the case, perhaps I see more. Ignoring the lenses I use to make my judgements and using the ones most people use is not the path I choose to take. This is because my perception of the world, eSwatini being my world, is slowly evolving, and, as I grow, I know that I must share what I see with everyone.

Let me explain what I mean. Two lab experts are examining the contents of blood of a sick person. By merely looking at the blood, also against the blood of a healthy person, everything appears normal. Then what could the problem be? For all they know, it’s just an inherent disease and the patient can never be cured. The blood is similar to one of a healthy person and anyone who stares at it agrees. To be thorough, they then decide to inspect every component of the person’s body, still with their naked eyes, everything seems standard, but what is wrong? One of the experts decides to use a microscope. The other says, “No, that is not how we should look at things. The only way to look at things is through our natural lenses [- our eyes]!” Whilst the expert is still convincing the one using a microscope, the one on the microscope yells, “I see something different!” They both take turns observing the tiny microorganisms present in the “sick blood” but not in the “healthy blood.” Their use of a microscope has opened new doors of perception. I am a firm believer of the idea that people have different world views. I am not implying that we have different realities but there are different lenses that people use to see the world. I have different lenses, with which I see eSwatini, than a person who does not recognize eSwatini as full of potential. I no longer see the fantastical tall buildings and America-like eSwatini. I lied, I do sometimes – twice on Sunday. Even if that is so, my perception has changed due to my exposure to places beyond eSwatini and uncommon ideas that I have learnt – whether right or wrong.

Probably the issues we are facing today in the country are symptoms of deeper problems. Taking a bottom up approach, I would say that the little things we are doing daily are holding us back as a country. There is a saying, “A chain is as strong as its weakest link.” If as emaSwati we hope to have a better economy, competent politicians, a cleaner environment and a healthy society, it is fair to also evaluate how individuals are doing in these areas. We cannot expect to build a strong economy if its people are only partaking in reckless spending instead of investing and saving. We cannot expect to have good leaders from a population that treat their lovers badly and a population that oppresses women and brutally treats other people. We, also, cannot expect a clean country if we carelessly litter all around our beautiful land, and make no effort in making our homes look good. Obviously, those issues are not fixed overnight and that is why we need full participation. As we address the bigger problems, let us keep the integrity we want to see the country move towards in our personal lives too. By doing so we can reduce the obstacles right in front of us. We can also realize our capabilities and the possibilities that await eSwatini if we take part in the journey of becoming a better country.

Learning from a lot of European countries, tall buildings are not the center of their status. Some countries such a Switzerland celebrate nature, the same nature eSwatini has! Some have strong cultures, just like we do! I strongly believe that at the core of everything is the confidence in themselves as people and their abilities. The ability to treat each other better; the ability for them to keep their lawns clean and beautiful; the ability to enjoy what they have. There evidently are more reasons developed countries are where they are today. They aren’t here because they thought everyone would laugh at them if one started picking litter at their nearest grocery store for free. There are more humble and attainable ways to encroach towards first world status.

There are many levels that each and every one of us can participate in making eSwatini better. We can have a dream but if we aren’t fired up to achieve it, we might as well throw it away and seal our fate. Practical steps an individual can take include the following: offer high quality service if you are a waiter, read – we need more educated and open-minded people, adopt a park to clean and take care of it, volunteer in a non-profit, and many more. For groups, start a business, actively work on your relationships, start a magazine, or create a donation app. These are random ideas that I had in my mind whilst typing but I think any of that would make eSwatini a little bit better and we will be on a highway to a better future. Just like the two lab experts, the tiny organisms were what differentiated the blood of a sick person and a healthy person. Remember, by also tackling some of the small issues we will have a healthy country that will no longer be limited by infection.
