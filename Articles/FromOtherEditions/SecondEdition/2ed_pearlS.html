SUCCESS IS THE CHILD OF SELF-DISCIPLINE |Pearl S.

Last edited: 8/2/2022

“Discipline is choosing between what you want now, and what you want the most.”
~Abraham Lincoln

Discipline is a word that is disappearing daily in the dialogue of today’s youth. I am watching our microwave generation failing to sustain anything they start. This is not the belt-beating-buttock type of discipline (referred to as punishment) but the discipline of training ones self to consistency in anything precisely self-discipline. Discipline is continuous and habitual, it is a way of life, a principle you live by to attain a specific result. It is my belief that we should learn self-discipline from our predecessors because, truth be told, the amount of discipline my grandparents had I could never compete with. This is not because of their experience but because of the acts of discipline they still carry out until this day. In most households truly you have made it if you manage to wake up consistently before your grandmother or grandfather you have made it in life. It would be an error on our part to overlook this because there lays the keys to accomplishment.

Do you not wonder how some of our parents and grandparents who did not go to school are holding seats in corporate settings. Their discipline ensured the mastery of their skills such that a university degrees that cannot be able to teach. Start now! If you think you will be able to sustain that business idea or venture in the future out of sheer will you must be boo-boo the fool. People who are able to keep things are those who understand perseverance and patience. If success was simply a matter of getting a formal education, we would all be eating its fruits. The people who are seated at the top are making money and taking decisions over your life while you sleep or while you keep postponing that assignment.

Self-discipline starts with making your bed right after you wake up, it starts with doing your homework on time and consistently, it starts with finally waking up in the morning to jog and keep on doing it even when it is not too convenient. Self-discipline starts with choosing to finish anything you start, it starts with overcoming all your excuses. If you do not start it not, it will be a struggle later. Honestly, I do not even have the time to be writing these articles, but I used to be that kid who always did their homework, no matter what. I am now uncomfortable not handing in anything I promised to do – discipline. Why bother? Well mainly because your success is your own responsibility. It takes one to push themselves past what they believed their limit to be and standout in the midst of their generation

Discipline is also associated with perseverance. Show me any person who holds their success but are without perseverance and I will show you a man who it is only a matter of time before they loose it. The ability to push towards your dreams despite excuses that have been rationalized by your mind is a fruit of discipline. Self-discipline teaches you how to value your dreams and goals. It takes time, effort, pain and effort to develop certain positive habits. When I started jogging, I went through muscle pain, joint pain, back pain, body pain, you name it. The stamina I have now has enabled other areas of dietary consciousness that keep me going. I keep at it because I know the price to pay to start again. The ability to keep getting up each time you fail and each time you are rejected comes from resilience. It takes an extra ordinary ability also called a ‘fighting spirit’. Our society is full of people who have a negative outlook on life, it will take you developing a mindset and sticking to it to be any different.

“The cost of discipline is always LESS than the Price of Regret. So, Self-discipline is the BIGGEST investment.”
~APJ Abdul Kala
