On Self-Esteem

Last edited: April 8, 2023

By Senchen Subba (from Nepal)

“I prefer to be true to myself, even at the hazard of incurring the ridicule of others, rather than to be false, and to incur my own abhorrence.” ― Frederick Douglass

Self-esteem is not a constant or something allotted to us at birth. Like all great human characteristics, self-esteem is malleable. It can be weakened and reduced, diluted and lost, but it can also be strengthened and heightened, developed and found. The beautiful thing about self-esteem is that it does not rely on external metrics, such as our appearance, wealth, family background, or skill set. This does not mean that a person with good self-esteem cannot be rich or skilled, but rather that good self-esteem leads to external metrics, not the other way around. Nor does it mean that everyone with high self-esteem is 'successful'. However, those with high self-esteem share one thing we all wish to have: satisfaction. I believe that self-esteem is a vital factor in determining the course of your life. Therefore, this writing will consist of three parts: what self-esteem is, why it is important, and what causes it to increase or decrease.

Firstly, what is self-esteem? I have found Nathaniel Branden's definition to be the most succinct and accurate. He defines self-esteem as 'the reputation we acquire with ourselves.' In other words, self-esteem is what we think of ourselves. Regardless of what others know or think, regardless of what they've seen or not seen, heard or not heard, we know everything about ourselves. There is nothing hidden from ourselves. Self-esteem is our truth, and it does not matter if anyone else knows, because we know, and that is enough.

Secondly, why is it important? Because we all know how we act determines our life, but fewer of us realize how we think impacts the way we act. For example, we act differently around people we love and respect. We give them the benefit of the doubt, encourage them, help them, and are willing to sacrifice for them. It's not even conscious, but we act with love and respect in everything we do around them. In turn, the person receiving our positive energy acts like a person worthy of love and respect: they don't have to prove anything to anyone. Not through the external metrics; like beauty, accomplishments, income or fame. Because they know they are enough. Conversely, imagine there is a person you do not love or respect. You treat them without consideration, without giving respect to their thoughts and desires, feelings, and well-being. You do not think they deserve anything good. The person treated without love and respect will be inclined to act like they do not deserve love or respect. In turn, they will have to find their 'love' and 'respect' through external metrics. There is no difference between the person in the example and the judgment we pass on ourselves. If we believe ourselves to be worthy of love and respect, we will act as such, and this will cause others to treat us accordingly. Vice versa, if we do not believe ourselves to be worthy of love and respect, we will act in ways that signal that, and others will respond accordingly.

Therefore, self-esteem is important because it determines how we feel on a day-to-day basis. It determines the activities we pursue and why we pursue them, whether we are excited by a challenge or fearful of failure, and whether we give our unique gifts to the world or hide them. It determines, when faced with life's ensuing challenges, whether we quit or we persevere. In short, self-esteem determines the trajectory of our life.

So, how do we increase self-esteem? I have found there to be two ways, both centered on the same principle: integrity. Simply put, we do what we know we should do and don't do what we know we should not do. It does not mean always being right but always doing what you know is right. It does not mean you cannot make mistakes but means you cannot knowingly make a mistake.

Firstly, do everything that you know you should be doing. This may involve cleaning your room, completing your tasks, having that difficult conversation, letting people know your opinions. It may involve making that big leap, making that big decision, holding yourself accountable, or being honest with yourself. It will be difficult and challenging, and you may feel embarrassed or scared. But the more resistance you feel, the more important it is to do them. Building self-esteem requires that we care more about our values than our reputation. More about our actions than what people think. For how can we respect someone, especially ourselves, when we know what's good for us but don't do it? How can we respect someone who cares more about what others think than what they think?

Secondly, act as if everything you do is fully visible to the world. If you engage in any activity or habit that would embarrass you if done in public, make a conscious effort to change that. Either become comfortable with the idea of other people witnessing it or avoid doing it altogether. Whether it is collecting stamps, liking teddy bears, or meditating, be at ease with others observing it. Don't force it on them, but don't actively hide it either. Being embarrassed about your actions or hobbies signifies that you are rejecting yourself, which is detrimental to your self-esteem. Of course, there are exceptions where privacy is expected, and we would act differently, but we all know what I'm talking about. Never let yourself take actions that you would be ashamed of if someone else found out, for that means you are also ashamed. Some part of you believes that action or inaction to be embarrassing. We must realize that even if no one knows about our embarrassment, we know, and that is more than enough.

In conclusion, I acknowledge how challenging it is to uphold these principles. I struggle with them on a daily basis. However, like all worthwhile endeavors in life, it takes time, effort, and persistence. But I know that it becomes easier with each passing day. If there is a more beautiful way to live life than with courage, persistence, love, and honesty, with self-esteem, I have yet to discover it. So start. Start small. Take that one step, do that one small thing you know you should do. Get up and do it right now.
