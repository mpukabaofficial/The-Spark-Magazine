AUTHENTICITY: Living According to your own truth

By Mlandvo-Sikhumbuzo kaMavimbela (contributing writer for The Spark Magazine)

Last edited: May 1, 2023

I grapple a little with the concept of authenticity, merely because we tend to abuse and misuse the term without having any fathoming of what this principle really means. Many of us have even had to expropriate this principle without even staying true to its D.N.A. Are you really authentic? In this ever fast-paced world of façades that often forces us to close the curtains on our true selves, how often do we betray our own authenticity? If you’d ask me that question, I’d ashamedly tell you I’ve contravened and affronted my own authentic state of being a lot more times than often. We suppress our true selves because society directly and obliviously conditions us to thrust aside who we really are and be in sequence with the doctrine of what a “normal” person should be like, in order to fit in. Ironically, the “misfits” and the rebels in society are the only calibre of people who are enlightened enough to live according to their own truth, being in perfect sync with the fundamental principle of authenticity.

Authenticity is more than just a paradigm. It is a state of being! Many brands and people alike tend to claim to be authentic when they are merely just following a ubiquitous trend. Being authentic is not constituted by pervasiveness. The major construct of the principle is the fact that authentic people never follow trends simply because they ache to be accepted. Authentic people are nonconformists who blaze their own trails and carve their own trends without any correlation of what the world expects of them to do. The day we realise that inauthenticity hinders our success and overall happiness, our lives will surely change! The sovereignty of our own happiness rests in our own hands, and authenticity is probably the most underrated key to substantial happiness. There’s so much fullness at our own disposal if only we realized that living our lives according to our own truth and veracity unlocks an unmatched higher power of spiritual enlightenment. Yes, authenticity is a spiritual course! It speaks to spaces and reaches to places that normal mannered people can never touch base at. An elevated pitch of higher vibration frequencies is unravelled every time you decide to challenge the normative status quo and settle to DO YOU authentically in all of your glory, unapologetically so! I implore you right now to let yourself loose of the clutches of pretentiousness standing in your way of experiencing this spiritual orgasm of being your own true authentic self.

Being inauthentic has grinding and devastating clouts on our mental health and overall psychological state of being. A research conducted by the health sciences department of the University of Essex a couple of years back revealed that toxic behavioural patterns of “pleasing” and the strong urge of human beings of wanting to “fit in” is one of the leading causes of depression. Often, we try so hard to force ourselves into spaces and realms where our presence is an unwelcome force. Our great desire for external affirmation and validation deludes us into thinking we need to presume a foreign façade in order to fit into those spaces and realms. I mean how often have we betrayed our own selves and worn a persona very different from who we authentically are in order to fit into those spaces? We even are willing to break and literally “cut off” parts of our beings in order to appease these realms in which our presence clearly is unwanted. Never lose your authenticity in any relation you form. Be it in relationships, friendships and even in corporate, never for any day, for whatever reason change and discard parts of yourself in order to assuage, conciliate, placate and to be accepted. Your authentic self is the highest form of currency that fuels all your virtues and definitely isn’t worth losing for anyone or anything.

Social institutions like family, education, religion and legislation plays a colossal role in perpetuating the subliminal toxic doctrine of inauthenticity that society acclimates us to. From the socialization stage, society trains and teaches us that it is utterly wrong to behave in a certain manner or be of a certain manner even if that “manner” is your natural authentic true self. We are taught that it is unreservedly erroneous and wrong to live your own truth to the fullest if it comes across as deviating and non-conforming to the conventional set standards of normality. We are taught that it is wrong to like certain colours if you are of a certain gender. That it is wrong to dress in a certain manner. This has led many of us to stay on the fence and live a life that is invasive and pretentious merely because it is acceptable to social institutions even if it means deterring from who we really and authentically are.

The other day I was baffled by Ugandan President; Mussoverni’s move of joining the ample list of African states criminalising an authentic state of being: same gender love. I am such a fan of love. A diehard fan of affection and like the fundamental human rights set by the United Nations, I also believe that anyone and everyone should have the liberal courtesy of loving and expressing companionship affection towards anyone they desire to regardless of gender and racial relations. It just hit me so hard how we live in a society that still frowns upon certain shades of love. It’s just unfortunate how the LGTQI+ community in Uganda and the likes has to now live a closet kind of life, shedding a huge chunk of their authenticity in order to appease the legislature that unapologetically dictates to them that they live in an inauthentic dud.

An authentic person is fearless. One who dares to contest the normative standards set by any social institution. President Mussoverni’s move of adopting the anti-gay bill has irked members of the minority in Uganda, many taking to the streets protesting in a solid stance to preserve their natural authentic state of being. An authentic individual is one who refuses to be swayed by any external force endeavouring to disaffiliate them from their true identity. I was also fascinated by the hijab issue by a multitude of women in the Islamic state of Iran. Now for decades, Iranian women and women generally in many Islamic states are compelled by law to wear hijabs over their faces in public settings. Even if you feel suffocated and suppressed by the hijab policy, you have no choice whatsoever as a woman but to put on the hijab at all times or else face excruciating punishment including ail time. Even against the backdrop of this pugnacious statute by the Islamic government of Iran, a legion of Iranian women have decided it’s time to discard of the hijab policy and flaunt their beautiful faces and hair in public. Even with such a strong militant government, patriarchal to the core, those Iranian women decided they now have a voice and it’s time to use it in order to disentangle their authentic state of being that has been systematically suppressed for eons. This realisation of strength and a voice by these Iranian women echoes the sentiments made earlier on that authenticity unlocks an unsullied and heightened level of enlightenment.

A perfect world, one worth living in, is the kind of world that places no captivity and infringement on our true selves but rather allows us the liberty to live our truth and authenticity. So never place a foray on your own authentic state of being. I’ll leave you with the cliché phrase; “be you”. Really though, be you!!! You are an embodiment of dazzling precision and candour if you embrace who you were truly meant to be.
