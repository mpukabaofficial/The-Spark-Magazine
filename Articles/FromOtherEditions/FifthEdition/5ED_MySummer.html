My Summer: Eswatini

Mphumelelo Khaba

Last edited: September 09, 2023

I arrived in eSwatini on August 10, 2023, about 2 years since I left. I pride myself of my country primarily because it was able to make me to be the person I am today. I feel like I have an obligation to give back or at least pay taxes for more people to have similar opportunities as me and better. To those who know me on a personal level know how much I believe in its potential. I have even written three articles expressing my disappointment after knowing that the dream vision 2022 was nullified. Coming back home was something that meant the world to me, seeing my mother and sister brought the biggest smile on my face and peace and comfort I haven’t felt in a while. Getting outside though, I wasn’t so happy. Do not get me wrong, there has been progress and development. I saw the Manzini to Sikhuphe Airport highway and it is phenomenal. It is world class – it even has solar lights! But the unemployment rate is disheartening, no scholarships and it is at a point where survival is nearing impossible. I have high standards for eSwatini and people are telling me to relax and just ride the wave. I cannot. I will not. I can’t dare do that. This is not a joke, emaSwati need to have a better life and for us to get there I believe that we should aim for high standards.

My arrival in my country was to visit my family and also investigate the state of my country. I did not need to look further from my family. Things we used to afford were now unattainable. Worker wages might have been increased but their standard of living is gradually worsening. What is causing that? What is it that we constantly do as emaSwati that is making us grow worse? Why is our middle class shrinking quicker than a plastic exposed to fire? Why are things objectively and subjectively worse? Are things worsening? What is happening? I do not know much, mainly because I do not live in the country 24/7, but I think something is going terribly wrong.

I have been wring a lot about the state of education in the country. Education is a weapon and if we do not sharpen it we become powerless. Eswatini is a small country and we need to realize that our land area poses us a lot of challenges when competing in the global market. Some countries plant a thousandth of their land and the area is as big as our country if not more. We cannot expect to compete with that. As we all know, the larger the production the cheaper the cost per unit. What makes it even worse is that we do not have access to the sea for trade – that is another one of many obstacles we need to gallop in the process of building our economy. But knowledge is limitless. Knowledge knows no bounds. If we invest in our education we stand a chance in being an important country in the global economy. But I do not know what is the problem. Is it the teachers? Are teachers hungry enough to bring us high level education or school has become a factory where as long as they tick the relevant boxes, it is all okay? But why would I even expect teachers to be passionate about their work if they can hardly afford anything. If the minds of teachers are constantly flooded by the frustration of losing their contracts in a year or two why would they spend time improving the very same thing that threatens their survival? I feel as if eSwatini is in a vicious cycle that can result is catastrophic results.

One of my biggest boasts as a Swazi, is knowing that our passport (as bad as it is in the global stage) is really good in the African continent. We can go to a lot of countries without a visa and we a likely to be allowed a visa. That may change real soon. Why? More emaSwati are leaving home and resorting to find other countries to live in. We may have the status of a peaceful country, it is becoming evident that it reminiscent of a cold war – something is happening under the hood. Are we seeing the mass migration of our intelligent brains? This is very bad. A hand is important – but a human can live without a hand but without a brain – life is impossible. Eswatini is slowly becoming a brainless country. We have a huge problem of brain drain. Before we had people go abroad, sharpen their skills, come back and cultivate in the land. Now, it is means to flee the country! I am very concerned. I am concerned for the country and its future. My brother is an engineer and hasn’t been hired in a long time. I have seen his work. I attest that he is one of the most intelligent minds in engineering and architecture and yet he is not hired. I'm not rigidly in favour of pure meritocracy. I believe in a balanced approach where opportunities aren't solely reserved for those who excel at something. Instead, they should be distributed fairly to ensure everyone's well-being. I also believe intelligent people should be awarded because people are good at different things, and whatever they are good at, they should be awarded for them. The country is not careful with its resources but chooses to waste them. Somethings up and it is not going to end well.

I love eSwatini because it is welcoming to any and every immigrant. Of course there nationals from foreign countries that face discrimination and racism but we have had a few cases if any that show that eSwatini is dangerous for immigrants. I want that to event get better for any national knowing that they can be in the country and it will be their home. However, I encountered a problem that disturbed me which was that I wanted to support a Swazi owned business and that was difficult. Why don’t emaSwati practise entrepreneurship? Are they not innovative enough? Or are the scales tipped? I advocate for everyone to be treated equally in any country despite anything. I do not do much maths now but I can see that there is something wrong if 9/10 businesses are not Swazi in a place with more than 90% Swazi population. Somethings really strange. Very strange.

We are a small country and we need to acknowledge that we can change greatly and become better. We cannot be heading straight off a cliff and act like everything is fine. Let us all be aware of what is at stake: this is real life! I am not implying that emaSwati aren’t doing anything but I am saying that radical action needs to be taken in improving the country for the better. This applies in all level: personally, in our communities and everyone. I urge those who have left the country with no intention to comeback to consider contributing in one way or another because our fellow emaSwati are not thriving but surviving a crisis – one that will need collective work.

Thank you for reading and please leave a comment below!
