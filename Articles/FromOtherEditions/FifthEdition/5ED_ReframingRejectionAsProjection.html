Reframing Rejection as Projection ...a necessary ingredient for growth

By Mlandvo-Sikhumbuzo kaMavimbela (contributing writer for The Spark Magazine)

Last edited: March 31, 2023

Do you remember the last time you felt the sting lavished by rejection? Was it that guy that never returned your calls? The friends who made plans without you? A father that never came around? Or was it that one person in your life that never saw your worth no matter how hard you tried to do everything and anything to impress them? Well, we all know that rejection excruciates, it unearths the greatest and peerless collective ounces of pain. Many times I have wallowed in the deep-cut wounds rejection has left me with, a countless number of times. Recently, something hit me and I realized that wallowing in the pits dug and crafted by rejection is not logical, it really isn’t. You see our lives can change if we only realized to ourselves that rejection isn’t an arch enemy but our friend.

The day we realize that rejection is not a measure of our impeccable value but a testament of another person’s inward turmoil, our lives will change! I plead to you to probe rejection and cast it as a psychological projection. Rejection is an apparatus that orchestrates a refocus of our fundamental objectives. It goes without any saying that we for many occasions, tend to use rejection as a price tag for our self-worth. A belligerent attack to our fragile self-esteems, eating away every atom of our physiques. And that is a very lethal manner of having a panoramic look at rejection. Today I want you to look at and use rejection as a fuelling mechanism for growth!

I love Nina Simone dearly and I try and implement some of the nuggets from her life story in my own life. I wonder if she still would have been one of history’s greatest musical starlets if a multitude of record labels didn’t reject her a countless number times before she finally hit the high note of stardom and got her big break. Do you ever sit and notice this cliché but realistic trend of success? How every success story has a painful genesis of rejection, countless rejections? I mean look at tech giant Apple. In a parallel universe, do you really think the IPhone would be such a powerhouse of a brand if the late pioneer; Steve Jobs didn’t face such several rejections from his own company? I do not think my political idol; Steve Biko would be such a historical maverick if his mission of Black Consciousness wasn’t rejected by a multitude of masses. All the iconic individuals mentioned here have one thing in common. Instead of languishing and wallowing in the pain spewed by rejection, they leverage those moments of rejection to their wholesome advantage.

The human brain and overall psychology is a distinctively powerful medley of tissues. Setting negative goals after getting rejected will without any doubt send equally negative impulses to the brain, and the brain as per its function will most definitely dispense negative reactions, and negativity will manifest itself all around you. I have noticed how each and every time I label myself as a “failure” or a “loser “ after getting rejected, my life responds to that negative label and everything around me begins to react and to affirm just how much of a bare failure and loser I really am. Winners aren’t really defined by the accolades and 1st place positions they have secured, in my opinion winners are sought after for the number of times they steadfastly never allowed their spark to fade, no matter how hard rejections have tried to dim their spark’s glow. Instead winners leverage rejection by using it as a concrete building block for success! Okay so your man breaks up with you today and you’re going to spend the next one million years asking yourself if you aren’t beautiful enough? Wallowing in self-pity and a diminished self-esteem? Or are you going to use that moment of rejection to grow as an individual and set ablaze new horizons? Choose the latter!

I will never forget the day I was primed to be amongst the school prefects back in high school. I was among the top achievers in my grade and my mates kept feeding me this crap that I was going to be selected as one of the new school prefects. Well names got called out, me and my friends waiting in baited breathes for my name to be called, I mean it was only logical for my name to be called out too. But guess what? My name was not even called out for class monitor. And what made things worse was the fact that two people whom I was academically superior to made it to the prefects’ list!! Assembly got dismissed and my shoulders got slumped with my head bowed so low in utter disappointment. With the little confidence and zero wisdom I had then, I spent the next years of my high school ruining my life. My marks suffered greatly and I occasionally got into fights, suspensions and detentions because I had decided to let that moment of rejection to define me. Instead of using that moment as fuel to work even harder, I set out negative goals from that moment of rejection. I decided that I was now a; Loser, a Failure and a Disappointment! And guess what? My life began to respond to those negative goals and everything that happened to me affirmed my theory that I was indeed one big loser. I blamed myself for not being “good enough” instead of acknowledging that I never made it to the prefects’ list because of the Principal’s preference and not because of my self-worth. Thank goodness for this recent enlightenment because I now know that my moments of rejection are never a reflection of whom and what I truly am. And I will never be defined by any of my past, present and future rejections. That high school rejection doesn’t overshadow my dream to one day own one of the biggest PR firms this continent has ever seen. And it doesn’t mean I am incapable of leading and managing people. That moment of rejection has zero bearing on my leadership skills.

See, I do not know the hurdle of rejection that you’re faced with at the moment. I’m unaware of the manner of rejection you have been punched in the face with, but what I do know is the fact that you aren’t defined by that current moment of rejection! Leverage that sting to your advantage. Use that rejection as motivation to continue scratching deep within yourself, deep till you find that passion. Find that passion and anchor it with purpose. Ignite your spark and let it burn so fiercely!
