The question of how can I succeed in spite of the influence of societal and economic disparities often troubles my mind. It often leads me to calculating what it is I have at my disposal and what I can do with it to achieve growth on a personal and economic level. To an almost discouraging level, I often do not find an answer to that question but more questions arise. There are times that I come up with theories that constantly are a result of my external environment. I often engage people who have similar views to mine and people who have, at times, opposing perspectives to my own. I enjoy talking to people who have different experiences to mine, in varying spheres of life, because they have knowledge in areas I am not familiar with. The main theme of these discussions I have is on the goal of education.

The Goal of Education

There is a dominant school of thought which indoctrinates us into believing that education is the key to success. To be clear, I do not dispute this nor do I believe that education is a standalone key to success, but I digress. There are barriers to education, on another level, there are barriers to good education. By barrier to education I mean barrier of entry into institutions of learning from preschool to tertiary. The question is, “If one cannot afford to enroll in these institutions, what can they do?” This question brought me to think more about the goal of education and whether it is possible to achieve it outside of a formal institution. If yes, does an individual have to wait to enter a ‘formal’ institution or they can find ways to reach their greatest potential without school.

“Reading furnishes the mind only with materials of knowledge; it is thinking that makes what we read ours.” ~John Locke

The goal of education is to impart knowledge and that will help develop people’s skill that can potentially open up opportunities for financial gain. So, when the opportunities for a formal education are dim, the alternative is to pursue knowledge using other means. This requires a level of discipline where one can sacrifice short term wants for greater future gains. As an example, if one has hundred Rands ($7) to spend on alcohol, entertainment in social media, clothes they do not need. If one affords to do this monthly or once every three months, they still can be able to use that amount to acquire more knowledge. I am also an example, I kept my hair short for a whole year, something foreign to me, in order to redirect the money, I used to use for elaborate hairstyles, into other areas. I can assure you, I am not one of those privileged and look good with short hair.

There are many more ways in which we can acquire more information to build our knowledge. We still have libraries in this day and age, not to mention the internet; furthermore, there is a television in most homes and with the most basic level subscription which has informative channels too. They might be boring, some may sleep through most of them; however, that knowledge one can get from those channels will accumulate and they might be more knowledgeable than those who sat through school. There all kinds of lessons: some provide business tips, some provide life hacks, and some provide skills that helps people optimize the resources they have at their disposal. To be honest, university gives its attendees a guide on which books to read then sends students to the internet to actually learn. Libraries also offer books that are not even prescribed that could help gain further knowledge.

There is another source of knowledge, and that is from those who have experience in life, whether they are deemed to have been successful or not. They should not be judged them for how they turned out, but one can identify what they can learn about their successes and failures. The aim is to know what made them persistent as they faced the obstacles on their way.

“There are three principal means of acquiring knowledge…observation of nature, reflection, and experimentation. Observation collects facts; reflection combines them; experimentation verifies the result of their combination.” ~Denis Diderot

As much as formal education is an advantage not it is not entirely detrimental to your success. The first thing that informs me that you can acquire more information is that you are reading this article – and it is online – imagine what more you can find that could be beneficial to you. I believe that everyone has the ability to change the direction of their lives and make an impact on the lives of people around them. Some people have been born in disadvantaged circumstances but have made it a point to use the opportunities right in front of them, making an impact in the world. The sure path to failure idleness, educated or not.
