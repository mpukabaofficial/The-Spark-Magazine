Courage Simplifies

Last edited: 19/02/2023

You’ve been walking for five kilometres; you know you don’t like hiking, but your friend is right next to you and that means everything is fine. You probably could have cycled with the red team, but your friend wanted you to walk with her. However, this time around, you were not necessarily willing to compromise but the thought of your friends being mad at you led you to break-up town. The monsters that haunted you, well inside your brain – of course, were too scary for you to wait 8 hours until you meet your friend. All this darkness happened within a second when she had said the words, “Go with me, it is going to be fun!” That is how things usually play out: you always choose to trade in a couple of unnecessary hours each day just to keep your friendship. For you, every time, it is always a good deal. You are just unsure of how much of your soul is left and how much of it are you still willing to give. What really is stopping you? Fear, to be precise, it is the lack of courage. But isn’t a person supposed to compromise for friendships? Can we really do everything that we want to do and keep healthy relationships? Well, I am not a relationships expert or close to that but what I do know is that one cannot compromise everything. I would assume that a person would like to be somewhat in control of their lives. However, this article is not about relationships; the idea I would like to draw your attention to is courage. For example, in this case, having the galls to say no, when the time is right. The valour of doing what you are terrified of. I am confident that this ability or skill has the potential to simplify everyone’s life giving them more command and a voice to what happens in it.

Courage can help us maintain our schedule relatively well

Discipline plays a vital role in following a schedule. People schedule in diverse ways: some use technology, whilst others have an estimate on their minds of what they should do. In any case, discipline intersects with courage in many ways. When some people are alone, they are able to go down their to-do list with ease but once you introduce friends and family in their environment they hardly cross off any task. They have discipline but their discipline is not anchored in courage. Once a person develops the courage to use the word “no,” they unlock new possibilities in their ability to stick to their schedule. An important concept to pay attention to is balance. When our environment changes, we need to expect results to change too. The aim is not to pretend that the company you now have does not exist; you are going to cross off less tasks in your to-do because you might have to spend time with your friends and family. The point here is to say yes to things that you find important in any standard you choose to use and also say no when you realize that nothing is getting done. That middle ground will keep your relationships healthier and your schedule healthy too. You have the power to influence your schedule when you courageously and consciously make decisions that work with what you want to achieve – simplifying your life.

Courage can help us navigate our relationships

Courage plays and significant role in relationships and our participation in them. I have always thought that people who are the infamous “yes mans” have an edge on relationship. Who wouldn’t like to have a person who conforms to whatever standard they set? It turns out that relationships are more complicated than that. There are still rumours that people like those who treat them badly; also, it is way complicated than that. There is no right answer to all these theories because they all have some merit to them. Some people have witnessed at some point their theory playout right in front of their eyes and they have a valid reason to assume that it is true. At the same time, there are errors in our perception because a person may have simplified an event they saw. Still, I am trying to show that there are no definitive rules to relationships. “Then if there aren’t rules to relationships how does courage make a difference?” one may ask. The idea of courage in relationships is intentional participation. Every person should, at bare minimum, actively participate in their relationships. It could mean that a person will tell the truth when they lose feelings for their partner; it could mean that it is time they confront that person who keeps on subduing or undermining them in a relationship; it could mean making a move on the special someone without having to spend months wondering if they like you back; it could definitely mean that you can say “no” if one does not want to do something. I know this is me projecting some of the things I have faced or are facing but I have also seen people fail to muster the courage to do what they should do. This ultimately leads to authenticity, which is a valuable trait in relationships. When we possess the power to pursue any option we desire, our choices become a genuine expression of our character. Unfettered by constraints, our selection reflects our true interests and passions. Thus, we embody authenticity through our unrestricted decision-making. If we unleash the trait of gallantry, we can be able to not regret “allowing” things to happen, but we will take responsibility of what the outcome of the relationship becomes.

The only pain we will endure is courage

Courage is painful and it defeats the human ambition for millions of years which is fear, but it is less painful than the opposite. Humans have used fear to drive them into dominating the world and that has been a remarkable success. In my opinion, in intra-relations of humans, fear is the worst tool to use. We are no longer threatened and in order for us to thrive, we should strive to be courageous. Beyond that courage is necessary because the pain induced in our minds by being a coward is relatively worse than the act of being courageous. This is definitely an opinion that I have: spinelessness always hurts my morale. Giving in when the time to execute is the worst strategy. It is shooting oneself in the foot. People do not act cowardly because they do not realize the importance of courage, but it is their means to be safe. Searching for comfort is a big deal for some people because they have been hurt by acts of courage. They may have lost friends and accumulated trauma. However, the consequence of allowing fear to run your life is losing choice. You will not be able to choose your job, your friends, you will not choose the size of you food, you will not choose the lifestyle you want. Worry torments the gutless – leading to a common result: regret! Regret is the hell space of the mind. It is not fun; it is you fighting against yourself. And the most dangerous thing to you, is yourself. Choosing to be courageous will enable you to endure the lesser hell. We cannot avoid the pain, but we can choose to be courageous.

Courage paves the path to our purpose

“If you had no fear, absolutely no fear what would you be doing right now? Is what you are doing a consequence of fear or choice?” I do not like the phrasing of these questions, but they do bring up important thoughts to anyone trying to respond to them. A better phrasing could be, “If you were more courageous, what would you be doing right now?” With this phrasing we realize that the aim is not to have no fear but to have courage to overcome the fears we already have. It gives the antidote to fear – in this case courage – which is more attainable than the absence of fear. We get held back if we do not have the courage to change our situations. One of the reasons we are held back is because of the way our society is structured, those who take bold moves are more likely to be punished to a point where they need to scramble even for their survival. Many, also, have done incredible things because they had the courage to pursue their greatest ambitions knowing the consequences. Pursuing our dreams is not only in large scale such as becoming an artist, but it is the daily tasks, we choose despite the potential consequences of making those decisions. Drawing from the introduction, where you do not say no to your friend because you are afraid of losing them. Our abilities to choose what we want is greatly reduced, and our ability to fulfil our goals is inexistent without courage. We need to be responsible for our choices but sometimes for things to change, drastic and reckless actions are necessary. If one really finds their purpose important, they might as well use courage to pave their way. The choices becomes simple. It no longer becomes a decision of whether or not will they do it but them doing it is inevitable. They must!

Courage: choice over fear

A running hypothesis I have is that fear is an antonym of choice. This is a hypothesis because I know it breaks down in some instances. However, it is true in a lot of other instances. Many people have been to school and sometimes the teacher says something they do not understand. It is always their right to ask for clarification. Guess how many people, because of timidity, decide not to ask for clarification? I say decide but we know they are too scared to ask. That particular decision is by default, it is consequential. There is absolutely nothing they can do about it. Nothing. If you begin to think about it, that is very – what is this word – sad! This does not happen only in their life; millions participate in this game. And the question always is: why? Why wouldn’t they want to know? Past that, the fact is they do not have the choice. “Fate” if I may, chooses for them and chooses the worst viable option for them. This results, as we have discussed above, to a hell space. If this does not motivate you to be more courageous this means that your terror runs deep. You might already be terrified by the thought of being courageous. However, the good news is everyone can become courageous. Everyone! It is not going to be easy, and it will be nerve-wracking at times but it sure will help us regain our choice – our freedom. That freedom will simplify our lives because we can mould our lives in any and every way we want to. Courage simplifies.

Think about it, there are so many cool things in your mind that you haven’t done because you did not have the guts to do them. You probably have a world of possibilities in your head, but your choices are leashed by fear. You may have complicated your life with the lack of decision; furthermore, you do not decide because you are afraid of the repercussions that result from the decision. In contrast, your inability to decided may not be because of you fearing the consequences of the choice, but it could be that you have been stripped off your freedom to decide by fear – just because. Spinelessness is not always rational. A breakthrough awaits you on the other side of fear. We aren’t trying to end fear, but we want to see ourselves triumph over fear without hesitation and with intention. It could be you today.
