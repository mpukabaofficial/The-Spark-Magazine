FROM THE SOCCER PITCH TO NAVIGATING THE FASHION WORLD:THE UNTOLD STORY OF MPENDULO NGIDI

By Dallas Day

Last edited: September 16, 2023

By far one of the most talented, industrious midfielders Eswatini has ever witnessed, most sports fundis would agree with me that Ngidi is a marvel to watch, his stylish displays in the soccer field makes one to not regret purchasing the matchday ticket. The fact that he is still yet to represent the senior national team with pride is still a mystery. It goes without any iota of doubt that the name “Mpendulo Ngidi” will forever be engraved in memories of soccer supporters especially those who hail from the Lubombo region after being the captain of the Titanic “Tambuti” ship which sailed into the MTN Premier league after an absence of over a decade in the premiership.

Ngidi is a living testimony of the adage which says “teach them young” as his illustrious career began at the age of 8 playing for Mhlaleni United under 13’s the rest is history. He has then since been the worst enemy of opposition defence as he terrorised them with defence splitting passes which made them to pry open like the biblical red sea at Moses and the israelites’ behest. He was captain fantastic of the dreadful Tambuti side which won the MTN first division league thus securing premiership status, they were also Mbabane Highlanders’ worst nightmare as they dehorned the bulls in a high intense Eswatini Bank quarter final fixture in 2017. He guided “bafana bemawolintji” as they are famously known donning the famous number 10 jersey worn by legends of the game from Pele to Maradona to Seven- time Balloon Dor winner Lionel Messi but most significantly Tsholofelo Teko “navigator” Modise whom Ngidi draws inspiration from hence he is also called the navigator. It goes without saying that when you think of Navigator you picture him on the soccer field delivering champagne passes, scoring monumental goal like the one which he scored on the final day in the 2017/18 season which ensured that Tambuti are champions. This well refined article in contrary will focus on his own clothing line “UNGA PRESS” and how went from being a soccer star into a fashion mogul.

INSPIRATION

Even though he is now engaged in the fashion space he is still married to the game as even the name of his fashion Brand “UNGA PRESS’ is a football term that means “don’t close the opponent quickly”. This language is peculiar to football stars. As in the book titled I can read with my eyes shut the legendary DR Seuss says “The more you read, the more things you will know. The more places you will go” Navigator drew motivation from reading the Premier Soccer League Galatico Teko Modise’s book titled “The Curse Of Teko Modise”. This shows that the power of reading can enlighten one to venture into life changing measures. It seems that Teko is a role model for Ngidi in all spheres of life thus it is of paramount importance to not just look up to someone but to take action in order to reach greater heights, motivation without action, love and a never die attitude is meaningless.

UNIQUENESS

UNGA PRESS ranges from sporty trendy outwear of high quality which makes it a must have for necessary social approval. The beautiful brand consists of stylish t-shirts, hats, sweaters, summer friendly shorts. One can easily spot the brand as unique as it is suitable for everyone despite age, gender, race and sexual orientation. This means that it is by far non discriminatory thus fitting like a glove in the modern fashion space. In true Rich Mnisi standards the brand offers seasonal clothing items like recently the summer clothing has been displayed to gear up for the highly anticipated summer season.

SUPPORT

This groundbreaking must have brand has been booming since it’s inception in 2020 all thanks to relatives, friends, soccer lovers who consumes it frequently. It is also vital to blow the horns for his team Tambuti FC which promotes personal development of their trusted soldiers, they are arguably the number one purchasers of the brand. Their players look absolutely classy in their UNGA PRESS sportswear during press briefings similar to the beautiful birds of Moroka in South Africa which proudly dons DRIP clothing which is owned by a black fashion mogul named Lekau Sehoana. This highlights how seriously African teams nowadays priorities individual development of their players and their financial security.

DRIVE

Ngidi’s secret success recipe is determination, respect and kindness in which he believes it can take one very far in life. Many would agree that we have seen former national treasures in the soccer scene became charity cases after retirement thus Ngidi’s best advice to present soccer players is that football is a short career it is best to invest in business for life after football. In the near future the Navigator aspires to navigate the international fashion space by storm and spread the beautiful UNGA PRESS distinct colours to the world at large. The lethal weapon in Tambuti’s arsenal who now doubles as the team’s Under 19’s gaffer may require sunglasses as the future looks brighter for him. Last but not least “Dream big it is possible” (Ngidi 2023)
