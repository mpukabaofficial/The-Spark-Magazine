Nomvuyo Dlamini: The Precious Gift That Keeps on Giving

By Mlandvo-Sikhumbuzo kaMavimbela (contributing writer for The Spark Magazine)

Last edited: July 12, 2023

Rupturing peripheries seems to be her life mantra. A visionary who has asserted her place amongst young people crafting their way to the pinnacle of success in the country. Businesswoman, CEO and corporate extraordinaire in the making. Going behind the archetype and fundamental ideologies of Nomvuyo Phetsile Precious Dlamini was a task I was so soaked up and ready for.

It’s another incomprehensible weather day in Manzini, a few rays of sunshine when you least expect them, next minute it immediately covers up with masses of clouds when you’re just enjoying the sunshine. I happen to be 20 minutes late for my interview with this maverick and in her true professional virtue; she’s fashionably 30 minutes early. First thing that leaves me in absolute awe with her is her distinct professionalism and her unapologetic respect for time and her punctuality. My late arrival and a few technical issues, much to her revulsion, throw her off the rails. Like the professional she is, she immediately gets grumpy. While we wait for our technical issues to be resolved, we decide to take a hopeless walk around the city. Like the ‘gentleman’ I am, I offer to carry her heavy-loaded bag while we walk but I’m told “don’t worry, I’m a big girl”. She immediately gets unshackled, all smiles and oh boy! She can definitely talk!! She takes me to parts of Manzini I am not acquainted with while offering me insightful conversations and a sneak-peak into her life. While still on our two feet voyage around Manzini, we happen to bump into her chief business competitor. Like old lovers they are instantly up in each other’s arms with such genuine adoration for each other. “You know, they’ve tried pitting us against each other”, she tells me. Though as competing industry leaders in the gifting service sector in the country, the two visibly have nothing but absolute love and respect for each other. After our preamble walk and a few window shopping trips, we finally get to settle down and our in-depth conversation begins with a few tear drop moments in between.

THE NOMVUYO STORY

Bred in Mhlambanyathi and later Mantabeni,the Siphocosini homegirl has always been a tester of new things from her earliest of years. The Usuthu Primary and KaBoyce high school alumnus has always been one not to shy away from any challenge since her bud years. She literally has played every sport in the almanac; from long shot, to swimming, to volleyball to cricket. “I once tried out basketball once in highschool and it didn’t work out”, she teases. Nomvuyo attributes her upbringing to the incomparable woman she is today. Her parents have played a peerless role in shaping the person she is today. With faith and the unchanging word of God being two of the arch principles Dlamini’s parents instilled in her from a young age. In an ever apologetic world, Dlamini remains steadfast and unwavering in her spirituality and accredits her love for God as a provocation for all her virtues. The Dlamini household surely did prepare Nomvuyo for the world and what was to come along the way. “My parents planted a fighting spirit in me”, she with such a wide smile on her beautiful face reminisces. Being the last born didn’t deter her parents from teaching her that she won’t always get what she wants and that failure and rejection, if responded to correctly, can be building blocks for a solid foundation. The life lessons inculcated to her in her household were definitely a beautiful prelude to the birth of her baby, a powerhouse in the making; Precious Treats.

PRECIOUS TREATS

Conceptualized in 2021 in the acme of the many stages of the COVID 19 pandemic, Dlamini saw a colossal gap in the market after she herself faced a pickle predicament on the afore of mother’s day. She was looking to bequest her mother with sentimental items in one gift pack. Browsing through the web one day, she stumbled upon the gifting service model and she immediately knew she wanted to do that for Mrs Dlamini. “I had zero resources, I was broke and I had no material, the only thing I had was a box of cornflakes”, Nomvuyo ruminates of her first ever Precious Treat. She set out to decorate the cornflakes box and splurged a few chocolates inside and that was Mrs Dlamini’s mother’s day gift from her last born that year with which she absolutely loved! That is when Nomvuyo knew that this had to be a mercenary venture. With zero to nothing start-up capital she had her work cut out for her. What was intellectualised in a moment of appreciation for her mother grew to become what is undoubtedly the go-to gifting service business in the country. With her own university student personal allowance, Dlamini was able to oil the wheels of her venture from a mere vision to an actual business. As a blossoming grassroots SME owner who is still on her way to prominence, Nomvuyo advises entrant small businesses against seeking loans without any solid fiscal start-up generation plan. Her paradigm is that entrant level business owners should first work on developing their own entrepreneurial identity before resorting to external start-up funding. “Fight for you to have your own start-up, do a deep background search before going out there and seeking a loan, and it is okay to start small. Sell those fatcakes, do whatever it takes for you to formulate an individual start-up for your business”, she abets. While profit is the sole purpose of any business venture, the Precious Treat executive broaches that the peaks of owning a business are not only constrained to mercenary and monetary horizons only. She attributes the incomparable sacrifices that business owners have to bear on a daily basis as one of the worthwhile reputes of being an SME captain.

Her business model is quite enthralling. She capitalizes and makes money out of something she herself is an admitted fan of; LOVE! And affection. As an all-time aficionado of love, Nomvuyo says she derives her biggest satisfaction from seeing the reaction on her client’s face whenever she delivers another bespoke Precious Treat. While still a small scale business on base level, Precious Treats has been a pioneer in the gifting service sector in the country. As an entrepreneur who is also an off-the-hook creative at heart and always pushing the periphery, Dlamini is not one to shy away from a business opportunity. Her business has tapped into the corporate realm as well. In February this year, Precious Treats joined forces with the Eswatini Tourism Group for a Valentine’s Day giveaway like no other. The move, aided by the group’s objective of promoting domestic tourism in the country saw Precious Treats being one of the crucial partners for the group’s valentine day’s promotion. Dlamini has stories for days to tell about the countless rejections she’s ever had to face as an SME owner. Instead of floundering in any collieries of the entrepreneurial rejection she has ever had to face, Nomvuyo uses any rejection she faces as an opportunity to do entrepreneurial self-introspection. Her partnership with Eswatini Tourism this year is one of the evident results of her unwavering tenacity to craft opportunities to broaden her horizons as an entrepreneur. Nomvuyo’s future plans for Precious Treats are unfathomable. While relentlessly striving to perfect and assert herself as a fully established entrepreneur, she has enormous plans for her business. Being a powerplayer in the Sub Saharan region and Southern hemisphere of the continent is one the long-term ambitions Nomvuyo has of her venture.

ENACTUS

Established in 1975 by renowned attorney Robert T. Davis, initially called the Students In Free Action(SIFE) . Enactus is a social entrepreneurship project, globally renowned for working with young thinkers, using business and innovation as two apparatus for solving social issues faced by the masses on a daily basis. Setting its armour in the country in 2002 in Uneswa Kwaluseni, Enactus has been helping young leaders in the country to reinvigorate and sharpen their entrepreneurial and problem solving skills in over 15 institutions including campuses in the country for the past 2 decades. One of Nomvuyo’s greatest accomplishments is being the CEO of the coveted ENACTUS Luyengo Campus. Stepping in a two deacades legacy of such stature as Enactus holds as a captain of the Luyengo ship, Dlamini had her work cut out for her. Being CEO of such a big organisation at such a young age was not something she could ever predetermine. Nomvuyo was nominated and handpicked on a mass-scale majority vote by her peers to steer this enormous ship. A move that irked some of her colleagues within the ranks of ENACTUS. She reflects on the time some members stepped down from ENACTUS the moment she was announced the new CEO simply because they did not trust the leadership of such a young woman. Nomvuyo acknowledges the fact that as a person, it is impossible to get everyone to like you. She has been called fake by people who find her “too good to be true” and positivity and the deep knowledge of her true self is how Nomvuyo rises about any form of negativity she stumbles upon,along her journey as a wearer of multiple hats.

It is an irrefutable fact that Nomvuyo’s term as CEO has garnered extraordinary rewards for the Enactus Luyengo Campus team. Under her impeccable leadership, the team won the National Enactus Competitions outperforming 14 fellow Enactus institution teams. A E20 000 cheque was one of the compliments that came with the National Championship accolade. The team was to represent the country at international level at the ENACTUS global competition in Puerto Rico, a trip which unfortunately didn’t see the light of day due to travel financing difficulties. Speaking of this monumental era that is undoubtedly one of the major groundbreaking achievements of the Enactus Luyengo Team in its entire existence, Dlamini attributes the dedication of her whole team as a main ingredient for their win. She states that her teammates make it so easy for her as a leader. A win that in Nomvuyo’s own words, came through a lot of sacrifice and a handful of sleepless nights for the team. The young leader is also beaming in pride as the Enactus Luyengo Team was selected as finalists in the Eswatini Communications Commission Innovation Challenge. At this point, there is no denying this young woman’s leadership prowess. One cannot begin to even fathom what the corporate future of this glowing young star looks like.

A beautiful story worth documenting, Nomvuyo’s life story is clearly one that will not be an ordinary cliché. Her relentless spirit and her beautiful aura that instantly lights up the dimmest of rooms is a blatant indicator that this is a powerhouse in the making. In a 24 hour clock day, the maverick is able to balance school, as a Bachelor Of Education in Consumer Science undergraduate student, a thriving CEO and a business owner of an SME. She admits that the pressure does get to her at times and the road can be a bit winding. “I put so much pressure on myself, I have suffocated myself and I have lost myself a few times” she discloses. The beautiful starlet reveals that she currently is on a beautiful journey of self-rediscovery. She accredits her solid support structure consisting of her loving and supportive family and friends as one of the cushions keeping her alive and bustling.

What’s more to say? The future undeniably looks blindingly bright for this young gem. And the pages and chapters in her success and failures book seem to be added in chunks everyday. And in her life-story, success seems to be the main protagonist. Nomvuyo’s Spark is one that will burn fiercely for quite a long time it seems, and this looks like just the beginning. And we at the Spark cannot wait to document every one of the monumental milestones she is yet to reach!
