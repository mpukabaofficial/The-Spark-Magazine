What is disheartening is that some of those people in dire situation also believe that they are less brilliant. All of this is total stupidity and lack of wisdom – especially to those who are in the privileged club. We are engulfed by a system that is ever guaranteeing that we stay in this absurdity. As contributing members of our human society, we need to ensure a sustainable future for humans and that can be achieved by the constant innovation in solving problems for our race. First of all, the guarantee that people will become successful is a strategy to keep the working class working tirelessly. But that is not the most sinister perspective of this part of the quote. Do I work? Yes. Do I work hard? That’s a different story, I know people who – when in my position – would totally burry me in hard work. I am a less than average worker and even if I was that hard working, it does not guarantee, justify, and qualify me to have a better life yet people are suffering. No human is supposed to be denied the ability to live a good and stable life. Also, people are not rewarded for how much they work, no matter what anyone can tell you. Have people worked hard and achieved success (whatever that means)? Yes, a lot. In the quote, success is the cherry on top! We have been sold the mindset of individualism that finds comfort in having a decent life. Nonsense. What about other people? Success has been the drive for greed, classism, and inhumane acts towards other fellow humans. One may say, “If I give everyone everything how will I survive?” The fact that we are okay for people to be poor, to suffer, or even die – when we know we could possibly make a positive change – is part of a bigger problem. I am one of those people. The arrogance is addictive. It is nothing short of a drug. Seeing people below us satisfies the thought that we are better. It is a constant addiction that feeds on people’s suffering and pain. We are constantly being sold on the idea that it is up to us to be rich and everyone can do it. Which is false. What if someone isn’t smart? What if a whole family isn’t smart? Everyone needs to have a roof over their head, by default; everyone needs food, clothes, and water, by default.
